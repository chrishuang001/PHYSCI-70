### Week 1: Final Project Proposal My artistic career peaked early in life when I was awarded possibly one of the most prestigious awards: Most Artistic in Middle School Admittedly, the competition for the boy recipient of this award at my small school was sparse… and the piece that won this award was a drawing which I traced (damn well mind you). But it was a great accomplishment nonetheless! ![image of middle school victory](./middle_school.png) My artistic skills have waned since then, but I firmly believe that is no excuse for not being able to create Mona Lisa’s out of thin air. After all, it would not be a sane world where just because you are a bad painter means you cannot create beautiful paintings. Introducing… Rembr-bot Harmenszoon van Rijn, the painting robot (name to be workshopped) ![image of robot painter stock](./robot_painter.jpeg) Part 1: A program, most likely needing to use some machine learning technique, takes an image and produces an artistic rendition of it. Then, outputs a series of colored strokes needed to create that rendition ![image of advanced AI](./advancedAI.jpeg) Part 2: A machine translates the computed series of strokes into real life paint strokes. Possible ideas include a more standard robotic arm programmed to do some inverse kinematics, a CNC machine to hold a paint brush, or some other custom solution. ![image of robot arm](./robotarm.gif) Part 3: Make a grand entrance into the world of high art, selling Rembr-bot’s paintings for millions of dollars. ![image of man with money](./money_man.jpeg)
